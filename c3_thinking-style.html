My thinking style and learning

Thinking styles are based on the idea that we use our intelligence differently. No thinking style is superior; they are simply different. Each style can be effective in its own way. The important thing is that we become more aware of which thinking style works best for us. Once we know our own style we can then analyze the others. This will help us understand other people better. It will make us more flexible.

At the end of personal thinking style quiz, much to my surprise, my thinking style was split 48-36 between Concrete Sequential and Abstract Random.I have scored over 30 in two thinking styles. This describes me as a divergent thinker. But as a dominant concrete sequential thinker, I always prefer order and follow step by step process of work. I feel comfortable when I work on a schedule, and a good day is when I can check everything off my "to do list". I like clearly ordered presentations and a quiet atmosphere.

As a Divergent thinker I have the ability to intentionally switch my approach if I find myself encountering a challenge related with the style I am using at that time.
